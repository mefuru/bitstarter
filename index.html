At University or College, you're given a class timetable on your first day. You know where you need to be, at what time and for how long. Although it can be a burden to attend a lecture at 9am on a Friday morning, you have structure and this provides discipline.

Discipline be difficult for on-line students, especially if they're studying multiple MOOCs simutaneously, have a job or other large commitments. Sometimes, I've had to rush through lectures and assignments to submit on time. For me, this defeats to purpose of MOOCs, so I plan to create a calendar that will create a study timetable and will allow students to get the most out of their on-line studies.

Give us three things and we will provide you with a timetable:

  • A list of your MOOCs
  • Your Calendar
  • Personal Preferences

We will give you a timetable for you to fit in your studies. If it seems like you've got too much on your plate, we will let you know straight away so you can plan accordingl. Timetables fits around existing events in your calendar - we will only schedule time in free time.

FAQ

What is a MOOC?
A MOOC is an online course aimed at large-scale interactive participation and open access via the web. In addition to traditional course materials such as videos, readings, and problem sets, MOOCs provide interactive user forums that help build a community for the students, professors, and teaching assistants. (Source: Wikipedia)
Where can I take MOOCs?
Check out Coursera, Udacity, Ed-X and Stanford's venture-lab.org
